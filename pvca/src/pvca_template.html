The Four-Part Test

How an Operating Partner should stress this

1 · Is the value real?

2 · Wouldn't sponsor procurement already capture it?

No. It sits outside {{ portfolio.incumbent_procurement }} and the GPO — the categories a national contract can't price (P&C on own loss history, tail logistics, deregulated energy).

3 · Does it recur into EBITDA?

Yes. Sourcing resets the run-rate — an initial step-down then recurring savings — so it underwrites as durable EBITDA, not a one-time rebate.

4 · Is it additive and exit-ready?

Performance-based and additive to existing procurement; no rip-and-replace. Clean, documented savings that survive diligence and show up in the exit multiple. {% if not centralized %}Structured to cascade opco-by-opco, so no central mandate is required to start.{% endif %}

Appendix A

Methodology

The estimate is outside-in and deliberately conservative — three inputs, transparently applied:

  • Enterprise revenue rolls up per-{{ portfolio.site_noun[:-1] }} revenue (verified filings where available, tier benchmarks otherwise).
  • Addressable indirect ≈ {{ pct(addressable_pct) }} of revenue — the non-clinical, non-labor spend a disciplined program can reach.
  • Recovery of 12–26% of addressable, by category, per the benchmark set in Appendix D.

Enterprise value applies the sponsor's own entry/target EV/EBITDA multiple to the recurring EBITDA uplift (§4). No multiple is assumed beyond the one the sponsor supplies.
